How deadly are the LA fires compared to California's wildfire history?

By Judy Zeng

Engulfed in flame, smoke and chaos, Southern California is facing some of the most destructive wildfires in Californian history. The Palisades and Eaton fires have become the third and fourth most destructive wildfires since fires were recorded in 1932.

The two most destructive Californian wildfires are Camp and Tubbs, which were caused by powerlines and electrical issues, according to the California Department of Forestry and Fire Protection.

Source: Wikipedia

The Palisades and Eaton fires are still developing.
